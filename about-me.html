about me

I am a Computer Science student at San Francisco State University, building a solid foundation in programming, data structures, and software development. My academic projects have allowed me to apply theory in practical way, such as developing a cache manager in C++ using advanced data structures, implementing a task management system in Java, and designing a responsive Norwegian cultural website with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

In the past, I built adaptability and communication skills through roles as a private tutor and caregiver, experiences that taught me the importance of patience, problem-solving, and collaboration across different environments. Currently, I am strengthening my technical expertise through coursework in programming methodology and software engineering principles, while also broadening my skills in algorithm design, debugging, and UI/UX development.

Looking ahead, I am eager to apply my knowledge in a hands-on setting and contribute to meaningful projects as a Computer Science intern. My goal is to grow as a software developer by gaining industry experience, working on innovative solutions, and collaborating with teams that value clean, efficient, and scalable code.
